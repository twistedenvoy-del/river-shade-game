üåä The Three Endings

Bad Ending: "Silence Claims Another"

How to reach: In the Haunt, choose to "Linger in the silence"

Meaning: Represents giving up and letting the darkness consume you without resistance.

True Alt Ending: "The River Remembers"

How to reach:

  1. Collect the River's Heart from the Cellar
  2. Visit the Bridge with at least 4 items and witness the full lore revelation
  3. Return to the Cellar with the River's Heart
  4. Listen to the whisper beneath the stone
  5. Follow the memory to unlock the Perfect Path
This ending unlocks the Perfect Path for subsequent playthroughs.

Perfect Ending: "Coexistence"

How to reach:

  1. Unlock the Perfect Path by completing the True Alt Ending
  2. Collect all 5 memory fragments in the Perfect Path
  3. Confront the Shade at the Bridge
  4. Make your choice: Listen or Resist (both lead to the same ending)
  5. Witness the awakening scene

Meaning: Learning to coexist with your darkness rather than fighting or fleeing it.

Unlocking the Perfect Path

You must first complete the True Alt Ending. Requirements:

  • Obtain the River's Heart
  • Investigate the Bridge with 4+ items
  • At least 4 items in inventory
  • Return to Cellar and listen to the whisper

Memory Fragment Locations (Perfect Path)

Memory 1: The Tavern (Fragment of Still Water)

Item needed: Fragment of Still Water (from Riverbank)

How to unlock: Visit the Riverbank first, take the coin. Return to the Tavern with the coin to trigger the memory.

Memory 2: The Riverbank (Fragment of Rust)

Item needed: Fragment of Rust (key from Town)

How to unlock: Visit Town, take the key. Return to Riverbank with the key to unlock the memory of the Unopened Door.

Memory 3: The Cellar (River's Heart + Journal)

Items needed: River's Heart (from Haunt) + Fragment of Memory (received here)

How to unlock: Get River's Heart from Haunt, then visit Cellar. Use the key on the door, then examine the paper with the Heart.

Memory 4: The Haunt (Shade's Mark)

Item needed: Shade's Mark (from Waterfront)

How to unlock: Complete Cellar memory to unlock Waterfront. Visit Waterfront to get the Mark, then return to Haunt.

Memory 5: The Town (Fragment of Memory/Journal)

Item needed: Fragment of Memory (received from Cellar)

How to unlock: After getting the journal from the Cellar memory, visit Town and examine the town square.

üíÄ Death & Awakening Narratives

Each death reveals more about the nature of the river and the Shade. The awakening scenes evolve based on how many times you've died:

  • First Death: Confusion and disorientation. You receive the Shade's Mark.
  • Second Death: Recognition. The Shade asks "Again?"
  • Third Death: Familiarity. You can't tell concern from mockery anymore.
  • Fourth Death: Weariness. Your reflection looks tired of waiting.
  • Fifth Death: Understanding. You see others trapped in the same cycle.
  • Sixth Death: Connection. The Shade admits it's afraid too.
  • Seventh Death: Acceptance. The Mark becomes a compass, not a wound.
To unlock the "Death Expert" achievement, experience all 7 unique awakening narratives.

Unique Death Locations

  • Riverbank: Reaching toward your reflection
  • Bridge: Investigating without items
  • Investigation Site: Exploring without protection
  • Haunt ‚Üí Bad Ending: Lingering in silence
  • Chapel (Town): Resting in isolation
  • Tavern: Listening to the whispers
  • Shade Encounter: Reaching toward or fleeing from the figure

üìú Understanding the Lore

What is the Shade?

The Shade is the manifestation of depression and self-doubt - the voice that tells you to give up, that you're not good enough, that the world would be better without you. It was once the "bridge itself" - the last soul to cross when the world forgot how to return.

What do the fragments represent?

  • Journal: The confession - acknowledging the problem
  • Coin: The bargain - the price of ignoring mental health
  • Key: The denial - doors you refused to open
  • Mark: The punishment - the weight of untreated illness
  • Heart: The drowning - the moment of crisis

What does the Perfect Ending mean?

The Perfect Ending represents learning to live with depression/BPD rather than being consumed by it or pretending it doesn't exist. The player character wakes up with loved ones, tells them about the nightmare, and when they see the Shade in the mirror, they don't run - they say "Welcome home, Shade." This is acceptance and coexistence with your mental health struggles.

The Cycle

Each playthrough represents a cycle of depression, death, and awakening. The more you play, the more you understand. The Shade isn't trying to hurt you - it's trapped in the same cycle, afraid of being abandoned. Only by gathering all the pieces of what was lost can you break free.
